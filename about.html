The Phoenix Park (Irish: Páirc an Fhionnuisce) is a large urban park in Dublin, Ireland, lying 2-4 km west of the city centre, north of the River Liffey.

Its 11 km perimeter wall encloses 707 hectares (1,750 acres) of recreational space. It includes large areas of grassland and tree-lined avenues, and since the 17th century has been home to a herd of wild fallow deer.

The Irish Government is lobbying UNESCO to have the park designated as a world heritage site.
